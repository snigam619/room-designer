How Global Sourcing Costs Work

Every product recommendation includes a full landed cost breakdown. Here's what each number means.

🏭
Unit Cost
The manufacturer's price at origin — what the factory charges before any shipping or duties.
📋
HS Code
Harmonized System code — a 6-digit international product classification used by customs worldwide to determine duty rates.
💸
Import Duty
Tax charged by US Customs on imported goods. Rates vary by HS code and country of origin — Chinese furniture currently carries a 25% tariff.
🚢
Freight Cost
Estimated ocean freight from the origin country to a US port, calculated per kilogram. Varies by distance and origin.
🧮
Landed Cost
The total cost to get the product to a US warehouse: Unit Cost + Duty + Freight. This is what a buyer actually pays before retail markup.
🌍
Origin Countries
Products are sourced from China, Vietnam, India, Indonesia, Poland, Italy, and more — each with different duty rates under US trade agreements.
